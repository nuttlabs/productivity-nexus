The WWWH Framework for Highly Effective AI Prompts

The quality of the responses you get from AI chatbots is largely a function of the quality of your prompts. The Who-What-Why-How Framework is carefully designed to help you provide all the information the chatbot needs, and in the format it'll best comprehend:

  • Who — Assign roles.
  • Why — Identify the problem and objective.
  • What — Specify the format.
  • How — Supply the method and resources.

Let's unpack it.

The Goal of AI Prompting

Think of AI chatbots as spheres containing information. To respond to your prompt, they can take any direction within that sphere across four dimensions.

The goal of prompting is to guide the AI to the location within the sphere of the most useful response.

By providing plenty of context, you point it in the right direction. By specifying constraints, you keep it from veering off course. The more abundant and specific your instruction, the closer the chatbot will land to your ideal outcome. That's why you want to provide the Who, What, Why, and How.

Not every rule applies to every prompt, but the more boxes you can check, the better the chatbot will serve your needs.

Holistic Strategies

First, let's cover a few general strategies that apply throughout your prompt.

Define your objective

Before you can guide the AI to your destination, it's important to articulate that destination for yourself. Consider your ideal output, then keep it in mind as you compose your prompt.

Example Outcomes
  • My document will be free of spelling or grammar errors.
  • I will fully understand recursion in JavaScript, including how and when to use it.
  • An email that conveys my frustration in a way that's respectful, uplifting, and positive for my relationship with the recipient.
  • A bullet list of the key points for a long-form article that allows me to describe the article as if I read it.
  • The termination requirements from a legal contract.

Instruct, don't ask.

I know it can feel like you're being rude, but because large language models essentially pick the most likely next word after your prompt, it's better to frame your prompt as instructions rather than questions.

  • Less effective: "What's the best way to fold a fitted sheet?"
  • More effective: "You will teach me the easiest way to fold a fitted sheet."

Create sections with XML.

AIs will also understand your prompts better if you break them into labeled sections. The best way to do that is using the XML syntax:

  • Mark the beginning of a section by placing its label in angled brackets: <Drafted Email>
  • Mark the end of the section the same way but with a forward slash or the word "End" before the label: <End Drafted Email>

It's especially important when providing material for the bot to reference, like the content it's correcting or examples to follow.

Now, let's get into the Who-Why-What-How.

Who — Assign roles.

For the who, you assign roles to the bot, yourself, and possibly the audience of the generated content.

The Bot's Role

The bot's role is most important. You should begin almost every prompt by assigning the bot a role. Be specific and include qualifiers for the context within the role.

Example Chatbot Roles
  • You are a modern marketing and branding professional within the tech industry. You're hip and original, never generic or cliche.
  • You are a lawyer specializing in the needs of individual business owners and independent contractors.
  • You are a high school computer science teacher specializing in font-end website development.
  • You are an excellent writer with the ability to make dull, complicated technological concepts interesting and easy to understand.
  • You are a professional copyeditor with expertise in English spelling and grammar.
  • You're excellent at finding unique, meaningful gifts online for recipients who seem to have everything they want already.

Your Role

In many cases, it's also helpful to give yourself a role. The chatbot is your assistant. Explain the context in which it's assisting you.

Example Roles for You
  • I'm an intermediate JavaScript developer honing my skills as I build applications through experimentation.
  • I'm a tech entrepreneur with a new business idea.
  • I'm a content creator who publishes video and written tutorials that help people leverage automation and AI to increase productivity and efficiency.

The Audience

If the chatbot's generated content is intended for an audience other than yourself, describe that audience.

Example Audiences
  • Ambitious business professionals aiming to leverage modern digital tools to increase efficiency and productivity. They use apps well but can't code. They only use graphical interfaces. They have no understanding of the technologies behind the apps they use.
  • New mothers concerned about the impact of emerging technologies on their children, like social media and AI.
  • My dad loves fly fishing but seems to have all the fishing accessories he wants. He'd most appreciate a fly fishing experience he can share with me.
  • High school students with natural design talent but no formal education on design principles or modern tools.

Why — Identify the objective.

The why is your ultimate objective.

The Problem

You can add helpful context by first explaining the problem you need to resolve. Why are you seeking support from the bot? Build on the role you assigned yourself.

Example Problems
  • I'm struggling to grasp the concept of recursion in JavaScript.
  • I have a coworker who repeatedly misses deadlines and delays communication at the expense of our client relationships.
  • I can't find a thoughtful gift my dad will enjoy, that conveys my appreciation for him.

Ideal Outcome

Share that ideal outcome you defined before beginning your prompt.

Example Outcomes
  • You will help me fully understand recursion in JavaScript, including how to use it and when it's useful.
  • You will draft an email that conveys my frustration in a way that's respectful, uplifting, and positive for my relationship with my coworker.
  • You will correct my document to be free of spelling and grammar errors.
  • You will create a bullet list of the key points in a longform article that allows me to describe the article as if I read it.
  • You will identify the termination requirements from a legal contract.

What — Specify the format.

Tell the bot what it will be generating in terms of its format and composition. Describe the look and feel of the response, like its length, layout, and tone.

Here, it's especially helpful to include examples. You can attach files or use that XML syntax.

Length Examples
  • It should be between 500–600 words. Your minimum is 500 words. Your maximum is 600 words.
  • The speech should last five minutes.
Tone Examples
  • Write in the style of Ernest Hemingway. It's marked by simplicity and brevity. Reference the attached examples.
  • Write in the style of the website Vox. It's smart but relatable, hip, and occasionally amusing. It makes complex concepts approachable and easy to understand. See my example below.
Layout Examples
  • Format your response as a bullet list. Each bullet will comprise a maximum of three sentences.
  • Create a table that compares electric cargo bikes. It will have nine columns:
    • Model
    • Brand
    • Price
    • Motor Watts
    • Battery Wh
    • Top Speed
    • Average Range
    • Pros
    • Cons

How — Supply a method and resources.

Lastly, tell the bot how to approach your request. Prescribe it a method and supply resources for it to reference.

Step-by-Step

Chatbots will interpret your requests more accurately if you break them into a sequence of steps.

Sample Steps: Decision-Making
  1. Clearly state the decision to be made.
  2. List the options or choices available.
  3. Outline the criteria for evaluating each option (e.g., cost, time, benefits).
  4. Compare the options against the criteria using a table format.
  5. Select the best option and explain the reasoning behind the choice.
Sample Steps: Teaching
  1. State the topic or concept to be explained.
  2. Provide a high-level overview of the topic, using simple language.
  3. Break the topic into its core components or subtopics.
  4. Use analogies or relatable examples to clarify each component.
  5. Summarize the explanation in one or two sentences, ensuring clarity.

Search the Web

Depending on the nature of your prompt, you may need up-to-date information. Remember, the data that AI is trained on cuts off at a certain point in the past. But increasingly, the chatbots are gaining access to the live web. Sometimes they'll search automatically, but if you know you need it, it's helpful to request it explicitly in your prompt.

Referential Resources

Lastly, provide the resources you've asked your chatbot to reference. That could be the content it's analyzing or examples to inspire the composition of the output.

You can attach the resources or paste them within XML tags.

Follow-Up

After the bot's initial response, you'll almost always want to refine it through follow-up requests.

Self-Correction

If you ask the bot to review its response to identify and correct any mistakes on its own, in many cases, it'll make corrections.

Specified Corrections

Many times, you'll spot errors yourself or want to make a correction or qualification to your request.

Edit the Original Prompt

In some cases, it's better to edit the original prompt with your qualified instruction, then have the bot regenerate its response. That keeps the unwanted response out of the conversation history.

Put It All Together

To come up with example prompts that use this framework, I fed it to ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude. Their responses were spot-on, so I've included one from each for your inspiration.

ChatGPT: Decision-Making Matrix

Who

You are a business consultant specializing in strategic decision-making for mid-level managers. I am a marketing manager evaluating which new software tool to adopt for automating our social media content scheduling. The output will be shared with my department to guide our decision.

Why

The problem is that we have three software options (Tool A, Tool B, Tool C), and I need to ensure we choose the one that best meets our needs within budget and time constraints. Your goal is to create a comparison table that evaluates each tool based on specific criteria.

What

Provide a table comparing the three tools with the following columns:

  • Tool Name
  • Price (monthly subscription)
  • User-Friendliness (rated 1–5)
  • Features (list the top 3 features)
  • Integration Capabilities (list compatible platforms)
  • Customer Support Quality (rated 1–5)

Write a 2–3 sentence summary at the end recommending the best option based on the data.

How

Follow these steps:

  1. Use a table format with all data points clearly filled in.
  2. Assume hypothetical but realistic data for any missing details.
  3. Summarize the key factors and explain your recommendation succinctly.

Gemini: Competitive Analysis

Who

You are a market research analyst with expertise in creating detailed competitive analyses. You are proficient in gathering and organizing data into easily understandable formats.

I am a business owner preparing to launch a new service. I need to understand my competitors strengths and weaknesses.

The audience is me and my team, in order to understand the competitive landscape.

Why

I need to quickly understand the competitive landscape for a specific service I'm launching, including key competitors, their strengths, and weaknesses.

You will create a comparison table that outlines key competitors in the specific market, evaluating their strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and key features. You will focus on a clear view of their overall positioning.

What

The response will be a table with the following columns: "Competitor Name," "Strengths," "Weaknesses," "Pricing Model," and "Key Features." There should be at least 3 listed competitors, and a summary in paragraph format after the table outlining any clear patterns or opportunities.

How
  1. Research leading competitors in my industry using web search.
  2. Gather and note each competitor’s key strengths, weaknesses, and value proposition.
  3. Organize the information into a table format.

Claude: Process Documentation

Who

You are a technical documentation specialist who excels at creating clear, actionable standard operating procedures (SOPs) that are easy to follow and implement.

I am an operations manager implementing a new customer onboarding process.

The audience is customer success team members who will be executing the onboarding process. They have varying levels of experience with our product.

Why

Our current onboarding process is inconsistent and lacks documentation, leading to varying customer experiences and longer time-to-value.

Create a detailed SOP document that standardizes our customer onboarding process.

What
  • Use clear, step-by-step instructions
  • Include decision trees for common variables
  • Add checkpoints and quality controls
  • Include estimated time for each step
  • Highlight required tools and resources
How
  1. Outline the process phases
  2. Detail each step within phases
  3. Include screenshots or diagrams
  4. Add troubleshooting guides
  5. Create checklists for each phase
  6. Include success metrics
